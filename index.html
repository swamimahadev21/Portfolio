07.Book Shelf

The Power of Your Subconscious Mind

Joseph Murphy

This timeless classic reveals the incredible potential hidden within our subconscious mind. Joseph Murphy explains how our thoughts and beliefs directly shape our reality, offering practical techniques such as visualization, affirmations, and gratitude to reprogram the mind for success. Through inspiring real-life stories, the book demonstrates how the subconscious can heal, solve problems, and attract opportunities. It teaches us to overcome fear, develop inner calm, break mental barriers, and make better decisions. By guiding readers to set clear goals, maintain optimism, and understand the mind-body connection, it becomes a complete blueprint for mental mastery and lifelong personal growth.

Attitude is Everything

Jeff Keller

Jeff Keller’s empowering guide emphasizes that our attitude is the foundation of all achievement. It shows how positive thinking opens doors to opportunities, transforms problems into stepping stones, and shapes emotions, actions, and results. Using relatable examples, Keller teaches self-talk techniques, responsibility for outcomes, and the courage to take risks. The book also highlights persistence, gratitude, surrounding oneself with positive influences, and focusing on solutions over problems. It inspires readers to dream big, build resilience, and lead with optimism, proving that a winning attitude can change the course of one’s life.

Think and Grow Rich

Napoleon Hill

Based on decades of studying successful people, Napoleon Hill’s masterpiece outlines a clear formula for wealth and achievement. Central to the book is the concept of a definite major purpose, backed by an unshakable burning desire and faith. Hill explains autosuggestion, specialized knowledge, imagination, organized planning, decisive action, and persistence as key principles. He warns against procrastination, emphasizes emotional control, and introduces the “mastermind” alliance for collective success. The book also teaches how to turn setbacks into opportunities and harness the subconscious mind for goal achievement, making it a timeless guide for turning dreams into reality.

Boots Belts Berets

Tanushree Podder

Set in the iconic National Defence Academy, this heartfelt and humorous narrative captures the transformation of young cadets into disciplined officers. Tanushree Podder paints vivid pictures of parade grounds at dawn, grueling physical drills, and the camaraderie that binds soldiers for life. Through lighthearted anecdotes and moments of deep pride, the book portrays the instructors’ tough love, the traditions of NDA, and the unbreakable spirit of teamwork. It’s a celebration of resilience, honor, and the privilege of wearing the uniform, leaving readers with immense respect for the Armed Forces.

Raja Shivchatrapati

Babasaheb Purandare

A monumental biography of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, this work brings alive his unmatched leadership, military brilliance, and unyielding devotion to Swarajya. It details his guerrilla tactics, naval innovations, daring fort captures, and diplomatic acumen. Babasaheb Purandare masterfully portrays Shivaji Maharaj’s vision, justice, and cultural preservation efforts. The narrative inspires with lessons in strategy, courage, planning, and moral integrity, making it not just a historical account but also a manual for leadership and resilience.

Yayati

V.S. Khandekar

A philosophical retelling of the Mahabharata’s Yayati, this Sahitya Akademi Award-winning novel explores desire, duty, and the consequences of human choices. The story follows King Yayati, cursed with old age, who exchanges it with his son to prolong his youth, only to discover the emptiness of endless pleasure. V.S. Khandekar uses mythology to reflect on ambition, love, loyalty, betrayal, and the fleeting nature of life. Through poetic storytelling, the novel delivers profound moral lessons about wisdom, detachment, and the true meaning of fulfillment.

The War of Independence – 1857

Vinayak Damodar Savarkar

This historic work offers a bold and detailed account of India’s first large-scale revolt against British rule, challenging the colonial narrative that dismissed it as a mere “mutiny.” Savarkar presents 1857 as a unified national struggle for independence, weaving together stories of bravery, sacrifice, and strategy from across the subcontinent. He delves into the causes of the uprising, from political oppression to cultural disrespect, and celebrates the heroes who fought with valor against a far more powerful enemy. The book reflects Savarkar’s deep patriotism and his belief in unity across regions and communities. Rich in detail and fervor, it stands as both a history lesson and a rallying cry for freedom.

Shyamchi Aai

Sane Guruji

A moving autobiographical novel, Shyamchi Aai is a tribute to the author’s mother and her values of love, sacrifice, and moral strength. Through tender childhood memories, Sane Guruji portrays the warmth of a mother’s unconditional care and her efforts to instill honesty, humility, and compassion in her children. The book reflects rural life in Maharashtra, the challenges of poverty, and the dignity of living with principles. It is both a heartfelt personal story and a guide to virtuous living, inspiring readers to cherish the selflessness of motherhood.

You Can Win

Shiv Khera

A motivational masterpiece, You Can Win focuses on building self-confidence, positive habits, and a results-driven mindset. Shiv Khera blends real-life examples, inspiring quotes, and practical exercises to help readers break free from limiting beliefs and take responsibility for their success. The book emphasizes integrity, perseverance, goal-setting, and continuous learning as the cornerstones of achievement. It inspires readers to turn setbacks into opportunities and live a life guided by strong moral values.

Yugpurush Shivaji

Dr. A. R. Kulkarni

In Yugpurush Shivaji, historian Dr. A. R. Kulkarni presents a deeply researched and balanced account of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj’s life, leadership, and legacy. The book examines his rise from a regional leader to a visionary ruler who laid the foundations of Swarajya through strategic foresight, administrative reforms, and military innovations. Kulkarni highlights Shivaji’s role as a unifier, his religious tolerance, and his ability to inspire loyalty among diverse communities. The narrative sheds light on his diplomatic acumen, guerrilla warfare mastery, and commitment to justice, painting him not just as a warrior king but as a statesman ahead of his time. It offers both historical accuracy and timeless leadership lessons for modern readers.
